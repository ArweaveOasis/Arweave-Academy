Arweave's Profit Sharing Communities

Arweave's Profit Sharing Communities (PSCs) introduce an innovative model for decentralized collaboration and innovation. By integrating Profit Sharing Tokens (PSTs) with decentralized governance, PSCs empower contributors to have a direct stake in the success of the projects they support.

Key Features of Arweave's Profit Sharing Communities

  • Profit Sharing Tokens (PSTs): PSTs are unique tokens issued by PSCs that grant holders rights to a portion of the profits generated by the community's application. These tokens incentivize active participation and align the interests of contributors with the project's success.
  • Decentralized Governance: PSCs operate with a decentralized governance structure, allowing token holders to influence decisions and direction. Voting power is often determined by the number of tokens held and the duration they are staked, promoting long-term commitment.
  • Open Participation: Contributors can engage with PSCs by completing tasks or providing services in exchange for PSTs. This model fosters a collaborative environment where individuals can choose projects that align with their skills and interests, promoting a fairer and more inclusive ecosystem.

Growth and Impact

Since their introduction, PSCs have experienced significant growth. In the first 100 days, nearly 30 startups were founded, with over $1 million in investments committed. Active communities have formed around these projects, demonstrating the viability and appeal of this model.

Conclusion

Arweave's Profit Sharing Communities represent a paradigm shift in how decentralized applications are developed and maintained. By aligning incentives through PSTs and decentralized governance, PSCs create a sustainable and collaborative ecosystem that benefits both developers and users.
